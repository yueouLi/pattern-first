# Starter Rubric: opinion-based video — v2 calibrated

For creators making 2-5 minute videos whose content is presenting an opinion / a concept / an article / a social phenomenon—in short, **your opinion itself is the product**, the visuals are just the carrier.

> **Important: this is the calibrated v2 formula**
> From a Chinese opinion-video creator (Douyin, 25+ published samples, T+3d/7d/8d data collected). **This is a real, active version, not a guess.** New creators with no data at all should use [opinion-video-zero.md](opinion-video-zero.md) (the v0 equal-weight placeholder).

---

## v2 composite formula (**calibrated**)

```
composite = (ER×1.5 + SR×1.5 + HP×1.5 + QL + NA + AB + SAT) / 8.5 × 2.0
```

Each dimension is a 0-5 integer. The composite ranges 0-10.

Weight rationale: ER / SR / HP are ×1.5 each, because emotion / social / hook are the current account's strongest propagation drivers. **These weights were set by intuition then partly validated by data**, not regression-fitted—your account needs to re-fit them.

---

## The 7 dimensions

### ER — Emotional Resonance (weight ×1.5)
*Can the script produce a **specific, nameable** emotion in the first 30 seconds?*

- **0** — pure information transfer; no emotional hook
- **3** — general resonance ("yeah, I've felt that too")
- **5** — sharp, specific self-recognition one is **somewhat unwilling to admit**. The viewer thinks "that's me, and I didn't want to admit it before"

5/5 anchor: crush self-deception, family obligation, internalized failure—anything that makes the viewer recognize themselves while feeling slightly uncomfortable.
0-point contrast: pure academic statement, abstract-concept papers.

### SR — Social Resonance (weight ×1.5)
*Does the script touch a current, contested, or structurally important social pattern?*

- **0** — purely personal / interpersonal level
- **3** — touches a recognized social phenomenon (workplace dynamics / dating / family) but with no new angle
- **5** — names a structural pattern the audience **recognizes but has no language for**

5/5 anchor: the reference creator's "housing price/top scorer"—the asymmetric impact of "a student's fall vs the top exam scorer" hits the intersection of three issues: class / education / the value of life.
0-point contrast: pure personal emotion (crush / relationship).

### HP — Hook Potential (weight ×1.5)
*Can the first 3 seconds force the viewer to watch for 30 seconds?*

- **0** — generic opening ("hi everyone, today let's talk about...")
- **3** — a concrete promise or counterintuitive assertion
- **5** — a concrete, vivid scene or assertion the viewer can't stop processing

Don't confuse HP with "sensationalism". A quiet but concrete opening ("some relationships don't end at a single moment—they shift while you're still inside them") can also be HP=5.

### QL — Quotable Lines (weight ×1.0)
*Are there at least 2-3 lines that can be screenshotted and spread as standalone text?*

- **0** — all narration; no aphorisms
- **3** — one memorable line at the end
- **5** — multiple standalone-usable lines, distributed across different parts (opening / middle / end)

> **Calibration caveat**: the reference creator's data shows **QL=5 isn't about "the number of punchlines"**, closer to "can each punchline survive independently in the comments / a screenshot / a friend's WeChat after leaving the video?"—this subtle definitional shift will be absorbed by a future v2.1 upgrade.

### NA — Narrativity (weight ×1.0)
*Is there a recognizable arc—setup, escalation, resolution—or a flat list of points?*

- **0** — list-style structure
- **3** — a loose main thread
- **5** — a tight three-act structure, the ending payoff planted in the opening

For opinion videos, NA matters less than for narrative videos. Treat it as a tiebreaker dimension, not a main driver.

> **Calibration caveat**: in the reference creator's data NA partly overlaps with HP—NA is cut in the v2.1 candidate version. Verify independently on your account.

### AB — Audience Breadth (weight ×1.0)
*How broad is the potential audience for this issue?*

- **0** — extremely niche (a professional circle / a single interest)
- **3** — medium (one group)
- **5** — universal (crush / family / work)

> **Calibration caveat**: AB was found to be a **misleading signal** in the reference creator's data—job-hunting is AB=5 but its share rate was 0.96% (the lowest). "Broad" doesn't equal "willing to share". The v2.1 candidate version will replace AB with TS (Topic Shareability). **Current v2 still keeps AB because v2.1 is still being verified.**

### SAT — Satire Depth (weight ×1.0)
*Does the script use multi-layer irony / parody format / self-referential mockery?*

- **0** — sincere and direct
- **3** — one layer of irony
- **5** — nested or self-referential irony (e.g. a script **about** a phenomenon, written **in the format of** that phenomenon)

If your channel takes the sincere route, give SAT a 3 as a placeholder, **don't** let it affect the ranking. If your channel takes the parody route, this dimension matters.

5/5 anchor: the reference creator's "housing price/top scorer"—three layers of irony (a fictional-country framework + academic-paper-form parody + self-referential acknowledgments section).

## v2.1 candidate dimensions (**not yet promoted**, for exploration only)

The two dimensions below showed independent predictive power on the reference creator's data, but haven't passed the full upgrade validation flow. **Don't use these two dimensions directly in your own startup phase**—the risk outweighs the benefit.

### MS — Memetic Shareability
*Can the audience reuse the author's sentence pattern?*

5/5 anchor: the "she's different" / "he's different" meme in the reference creator's "stop expecting"—the audience proactively applied the sentence pattern to self-deprecate, 2266 likes topping the chart, 12+ variants throughout.
0-point contrast: a punchline quoted verbatim but **not reused to make new sentences**—the "no-need information supply" in the reference creator's "who asked you", 362 likes topping but only quotable.

Why it matters: a quoted punchline drives **single-shot propagation**. A reusable template drives **generative propagation**—the comment itself becomes a performance, and each performance is free propagation.

### TS — Topic Shareability
*If the audience reposts this video, does the repost itself expose a situation they don't want to expose?*

- **1** — reposting equals admitting something painful or stigmatized (job-hunting, being offended, recently broke up and acquaintances can guess who)
- **3** — reposting is neutral
- **5** — reposting **is itself a performance** (collective ranting / in-group jargon / value-stance signaling / safe self-deprecation)

Why it matters: issue breadth ≠ traffic. A "universal" but socially-exposing issue (e.g. job-hunting) can't beat a "niche" but share-safe issue (e.g. ranting about the boss). **Shareability is the constraining variable, not breadth.**

> **If you want to try v2.1**: run 5 v2 calibration pieces + proactively collect "trial scores" for MS and TS as candidate-dimension data. After enough evidence (≥3 samples of support), go through the full upgrade flow via `/cheat-bump`.

## Bucket prediction (**only for calibration-period users with an existing follower base**)

> ⚠️ **New creators shouldn't use these absolute buckets.** A 0-follower account's video typically lands in the 100-1000 plays range—copying the table below makes every prediction land in "bottom 99%".
> Cold-start users should use the **ratio-bucket scheme** of [opinion-video-zero.md](opinion-video-zero.md).
> Calibration users (≥5 pieces with data) auto-derive after `/cheat-init` asks for the baseline.
> The table below is the actual fit of the "reference creator" (Chinese Douyin opinion video, ~10k followers, 25+ published samples)—**not a universal constant**.

Don't predict a specific number. Predict a **bucket** + probability distribution.

| Bucket | Range (plays) | The reference creator's prior probability |
|---|---|---|
| bottom | <50k | 5% |
| baseline | 50k-300k | 35% |
| hit | 300k-1M | 45% |
| breakout | 1M-1.5M | 12% |
| phenomenal | >1.5M | 3% |

**Important**: set bucket boundaries by your own account's percentiles. Your "breakout" might be 50k (reference-creator level) or 5k (newcomer level) or 5M (big-V level). Anyone copying the table above directly gets perpetually high / low predictions.

After calibrating ≥5 pieces, run `/cheat-bump --bucket-only` to let the system auto-derive based on your actual distribution.

Each prediction should give:
- **The most likely bucket** (the headline call)
- **The central** (your point estimate within that bucket)
- **A probability distribution over all buckets** (must sum to 100%)

## T+N retro window

Default: **T+3d**.

Why 3 days rather than 5 / 7:
- Most short-video platforms' algorithmic-distribution decisions are basically done within 72 hours
- Waiting longer introduces noise (slow long tail, secondary recommendations) without adding signal
- T+3d lets you **run a calibration loop once a week**, keeping the rubric responsive

Override only when you have evidence your platform behaves differently. Remember to write the override into `rubric_notes.md`.

## Anchors (anchor samples)

When scoring a new draft, find 2-4 old samples with a close composite, list their actuals. **Anchor comparison catches errors the formula can't.**

The anchor set is accumulated as you publish. **Before you have ≥5 calibrated pieces, anchors are unavailable—this is normal.** In cold-start skip the anchor comparison, but mark `Anchor comparison: N/A (cold-start)` in the prediction file.

## Where this rubric is destined to be wrong (known limitations)

Failure modes exposed by the reference creator's data. Watch for them in your own data too:

1. **Duration**: no dimension in the rubric captures script duration. A 4-minute script pays a completion-rate price. Track completion rate separately
2. **Issue timeliness / platform sentiment**: algorithmic preference shifts. A topic pushed last month may be suppressed this month
3. **Concept density**: cramming too many self-coined terms into one script (4+ abbreviations, multiple frameworks) compresses comprehension load and hurts completion rate. The current rubric doesn't penalize this
4. **MS / TS interaction** (v2.1 candidate dimensions): each may look OK alone, but a true viral hit needs both ≥4. A single dimension at the extreme is rarely enough

## Scoring-speed cheat sheet

Set yourself a time when scoring a new draft. The point is to **give a judgment that can be retro'd**, not to write a thesis.

- **30 seconds per dimension** is the right pace. If a dimension makes you want to exceed this, you're rationalizing, not scoring
- **Trust your first integer.** If you keep flip-flopping between 3 and 4, write 3 + a note. After 5 scorings the rubric tells you whether you're systematically low
- **Don't look at anchors before scoring.** If you check "what did 'stop expecting' score" before scoring, you're already anchored. **Blind-score first, then compare.**
