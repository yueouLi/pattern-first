## ⚠️ Patterns are a toolbox, not a mold (core meta principle)

Any pattern is **one of the paths reverse-engineered from your published samples**—not a template that should be applied by default.

If you use the same "3-section + acknowledgments + interaction hook" every time, your account's ceiling is just "a stably-made medium hit". **A true viral hit comes from structural innovation**—things outside the patterns.

Before writing, **always answer first**: what is this draft's strongest attribute? Then decide which pattern to use.

## Generic pattern library (**initial abstract skeleton, fill in specifics after retros**)

### Pattern 1: opening hook (first 3 seconds)

**Principle**: never use "in recent years / hi everyone / today let's talk about".

**Known-effective hook types** (reference—which one suits your account best needs testing):

- **A. Second-person scene immersion → reversal** (applies: the original has a concrete vehicle / experimental subject / case)
- **B. Parody-of-the-original-style impact statement** (applies: the original has a sharp core assertion)
- **C. Throw data directly / a reversal observation** (applies: the original has a strong data point + contrast)

> Which works best on your account, cheat-retro gradually fills in after retros.

---

### Pattern 2: number of body sections + how to cut

**Principle**: avoid 4-7 parallel concepts. Generally compress to **3**—pick the most nameable + most reusable.

> This is a **rule of thumb**—not absolute. Some channels (e.g. tutorial-type) suit more sections. Go by your account's testing.

---

### Pattern 3: stripping / keeping the academic packaging (if applicable)

**Principle**: can the audience **restate/reuse** this thing in the comments?

| Generally keep | Generally strip |
|---|---|
| self-coined concept names (a source of high MS) | model names (abbreviations longer than 3 chars) |
| key precise numbers | sample-size build-up |
| the acknowledgments section (if it's itself the MVP line) | literature review |
| reversal data points | limitations discussion |
| punchline sections of the author's original words | experiment-procedure details (unless they're the hook) |

> If you don't do academic / paper-explanation type content, this pattern doesn't apply—you can delete it.

---

### Pattern 4: emotional marker nodes

**Principle**: in well-performing scripts the first-person "I" appears 0-2 times, **always at an emotional climax**—giving the audience a "time to resonate" cue. Each time "I" appears, it must be immediately followed by a high-intensity information point. **Don't say an empty "I think".**

---

### Pattern 5: dual-voice structure (author + user narration)

**Principle** (if you do content explanation / commentary):

- The author's words introduce the **framework**
- Your words do the **grounded translation** ("the working-person's version" / "meaning" / "in other words" / "in plain words")
- Your words do the **emotional marking** ("what broke me most" / "the most reversal-y part" / "even scarier")

**Never** let a section go more than 4 lines without a user grounding word.

> A channel that doesn't explain others' content (pure personal-opinion expression) doesn't apply this pattern.

---

### Pattern 6: the acknowledgments section / MVP line aren't mandatory

**Principle**: only fully quote the acknowledgments section or outro when it **is itself the MVP line** (i.e. can stand alone as a meme). Otherwise replace it with the closing core punchline.

The acknowledgments section is the core propagation vehicle of **some samples**, but not a killer move—**the MVP line is**.

> Does your account have a signature outro? Should you build one?

---

### Pattern 7: sentence length / paragraph rhythm (**the most overlooked**)

**Write drafts in paragraph form** (each paragraph 100-300 chars, commas, periods, natural sentences), **don't write line-by-line subtitle format**—that's the subtitle the editor auto-breaks after the user films, **not the form at writing time**.

- **Writing form** (scripts/<id>.md / videos/<id>/script.md): full paragraphs
- **Subtitle form** (after the video is filmed): the editor auto-breaks into 5-15 chars / line
- cheat-seed **only outputs paragraph form** when writing a draft

**Sentence rhythm within a paragraph**:
- **Short sentences predominate**—avoid compound sentences, avoid literary connectors
- Use periods + commas normally, don't fear punctuation
- **Use more tone particles** (related to your account's style)

---

### Pattern 8: vocabulary style

**Principle**: **you should build your own vocabulary library**—it's key to brand recognition.

| Category | Use | Your vocabulary |
|---|---|---|
| grounded translation | translate abstract concepts into everyday speech | (to be filled) |
| reversal markers | "but" / "even scarier" etc. | (to be filled) |
| emotional markers | "broke me" / "scalp tingling" etc. | (to be filled) |
| rhythm words | sentence-end "ah / just / you see" | (to be filled) |

**Avoid** (universal):
- thesis-style connectors: "secondly" / "this study" / "in summary" / "therefore"
- adjective pile-up
- long, drawn-out modifiers

### Pattern 9: duration target

**Principle**: your typical_duration_seconds determines the word count (set at cheat-init).

**Don't exceed typical_duration × 1.2**—the longer past the threshold, the bigger the completion-rate price.
